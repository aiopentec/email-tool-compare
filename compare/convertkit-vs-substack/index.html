ConvertKit vs Substack: Detailed Comparison for Creators

ConvertKit and Substack are both popular email platforms designed for creators, but they serve different needs. ConvertKit excels as a comprehensive marketing automation tool with landing pages and commerce features, while Substack specializes in monetizing newsletters through direct reader payments. Your choice depends on whether you need advanced marketing tools or a platform optimized for paid subscriptions.

Frequently asked questions

Is ConvertKit better than Substack?

It depends on your goals. ConvertKit is better for creators who need comprehensive marketing automation, landing pages, and e-commerce tools. Substack is better for writers who want a simple platform optimized for monetizing subscriptions directly. ConvertKit has a higher G2 rating (4.4 vs 4.0), suggesting stronger overall satisfaction among marketing-focused users.

Which is cheaper, ConvertKit or Substack?

Substack is cheaper if you're monetizing through paid subscriptions, as it takes only 10% of revenue with no fixed fees. ConvertKit starts at $29/month for paid plans, though the free tier supports up to 1,000 subscribers. For creators earning significant subscription revenue, Substack's revenue-share model typically costs less.

Can I switch from Substack to ConvertKit?

Yes, you can export your subscriber list from Substack and import it into ConvertKit. However, you'll lose Substack's built-in paid subscription system and will need to set up alternative monetization or use ConvertKit's commerce features. The transition is straightforward, but your paid subscription workflow will need reconfiguration.
