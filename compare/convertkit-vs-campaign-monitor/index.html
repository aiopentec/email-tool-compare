ConvertKit vs Campaign Monitor: Detailed Comparison

ConvertKit and Campaign Monitor are both established email marketing platforms, but they serve different audiences. ConvertKit targets creators and course sellers with creator-focused features, while Campaign Monitor focuses on agencies and teams managing multiple brands. Choosing between them depends on your business model, team size, and feature priorities.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is ConvertKit better than Campaign Monitor?

It depends on your use case. ConvertKit scores higher on review sites (4.4 vs 4.1 on G2) and is purpose-built for creators with features like landing pages and commerce. Campaign Monitor excels for agencies with multi-brand management. Neither is objectively 'better'—they're optimized for different audiences.

Which is cheaper, ConvertKit or Campaign Monitor?

Campaign Monitor starts at $11/month, making it cheaper upfront. However, ConvertKit offers a free tier for up to 1,000 subscribers, which is ideal if you're just starting out. Once you exceed 1,000 subscribers, ConvertKit's $29/month plan becomes the paid entry point.

Can I switch from Campaign Monitor to ConvertKit?

Yes, both platforms support list imports, so migrating your subscribers is straightforward. ConvertKit's interface is particularly user-friendly for creators switching from other platforms. Both offer customer support to help with the migration process.
