Substack vs Buttondown: Side-by-Side Comparison (2025)

Choosing between Substack and Buttondown comes down to your specific use case. Both are legitimate email marketing platforms, but they serve different audiences. Substack is built for writers monetising directly from subscribers, while Buttondown targets developers and technical writers who prefer markdown. Frequently Asked Questions

Is Substack better than Buttondown?

It depends on your use case. Substack wins for writers monetising directly from subscribers, while Buttondown is stronger for developers and technical writers who prefer markdown. Neither is objectively better — the right choice depends on your specific needs.

Which is cheaper, Substack or Buttondown?

Substack starts at a lower price point ($0/mo vs $9/mo). However, pricing scales with subscriber count, so the total cost depends on your list size.

Can I switch from Buttondown to Substack?

Yes, switching from Buttondown to Substack is possible. Most platforms support CSV import/export for subscriber lists. Plan for some time to recreate automations and templates, as these rarely transfer directly between platforms.
