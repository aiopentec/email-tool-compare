Frequently asked questions

Is Drip better than Campaign Monitor?

Neither is universally better—it depends on your needs. Drip excels for e-commerce businesses needing revenue attribution and SMS, earning a higher G2 rating of 4.4. Campaign Monitor is better for agencies managing multiple brands with a lower starting price of $11/month. Choose based on your specific use case.

Which is cheaper, Drip or Campaign Monitor?

Campaign Monitor is significantly cheaper, starting at $11/month compared to Drip's $39/month. However, Drip's additional e-commerce features and SMS capabilities may justify the higher cost for online retailers. Consider your feature requirements when evaluating total cost of ownership.

Can I switch from Campaign Monitor to Drip?

Yes, most email marketing platforms support importing your subscriber lists and campaign data. Both Drip and Campaign Monitor allow data exports and migrations, though you'll need to manually recreate automations and templates. Plan your migration during a low-activity period to minimize disruption.
