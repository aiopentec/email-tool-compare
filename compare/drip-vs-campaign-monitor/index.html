Frequently Asked Questions

Is Drip better than Campaign Monitor?

It depends on your needs. Drip is better for e-commerce businesses needing advanced automation and revenue tracking, earning a higher G2 rating (4.4 vs 4.1). Campaign Monitor excels for agencies managing multiple brands with lower pricing and superior design tools. Neither is universally 'better'—it's about fit.

Which is cheaper, Drip or Campaign Monitor?

Campaign Monitor is significantly cheaper, starting at $11/month compared to Drip's $39/month. This makes Campaign Monitor the better choice for budget-conscious businesses or those just starting email marketing. However, Drip's higher price reflects its e-commerce-specific features.

Can I switch from Campaign Monitor to Drip?

Yes, both platforms typically support importing contact lists and email templates. The migration process is relatively straightforward, though you may need to recreate automation workflows since each platform has different automation builders. Most users report the switch takes a few hours to a day depending on complexity.
