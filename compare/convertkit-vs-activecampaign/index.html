ConvertKit vs ActiveCampaign: Full Comparison

ConvertKit and ActiveCampaign serve different audiences in the email marketing space. ConvertKit is purpose-built for creators and content sellers, while ActiveCampaign offers comprehensive CRM and automation for larger businesses. Choosing between them depends on your business model, team size, and required feature depth.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is ConvertKit better than ActiveCampaign?

Neither is objectively better—it depends on your needs. ConvertKit excels for creators with its intuitive design and built-in tools like landing pages and commerce features. ActiveCampaign is better for businesses needing advanced CRM, lead scoring, and complex automation workflows. ConvertKit has stronger Trustpilot ratings (3.2 vs 4.1), but ActiveCampaign ranks slightly higher on G2 (4.5 vs 4.4).

Which is cheaper, ConvertKit or ActiveCampaign?

ConvertKit offers a free tier supporting up to 1,000 subscribers, making it more affordable for beginners. ActiveCampaign's lowest tier starts at $15/month with no free option. However, ActiveCampaign's base price is lower if you're ready to pay. For growing teams, ConvertKit's paid plans start at $29/month, while ActiveCampaign's features scale with subscriber count.

Can I switch from ActiveCampaign to ConvertKit?

Yes, you can migrate your subscriber lists and email sequences, though the process requires manual work or third-party migration tools. ConvertKit's simpler feature set may require restructuring complex ActiveCampaign workflows. Both platforms support CSV imports, making data transfer possible. Check with ConvertKit support for migration assistance to ensure your data transfers cleanly.
