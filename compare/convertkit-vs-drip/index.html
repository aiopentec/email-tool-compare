ConvertKit vs Drip: Detailed Comparison for Email Marketers

ConvertKit and Drip are both established email marketing platforms launched in 2013, but they serve different audiences. ConvertKit targets creators and course sellers with an accessible free tier, while Drip specializes in ecommerce integration with advanced revenue tracking. Choosing between them depends on your business model and technical needs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is ConvertKit better than Drip?

It depends on your use case. ConvertKit is better for creators and has a free tier, making it ideal for starting out. Drip is superior for ecommerce businesses needing sophisticated revenue tracking and store integration. Both have identical G2 ratings (4.4), so choice comes down to your specific features and budget needs.

Which is cheaper, ConvertKit or Drip?

ConvertKit is significantly cheaper if you're starting out, thanks to its free tier supporting up to 1,000 subscribers. Once you upgrade, ConvertKit costs $29/month while Drip starts at $39/month. For ecommerce, Drip's pricing may be justified by its specialized features.

Can I switch from Drip to ConvertKit?

Yes, both platforms support standard email export and import functions. You'll be able to migrate your subscriber list and historical data. However, you'll need to manually recreate automation workflows and sequences, as they don't directly transfer between platforms.
