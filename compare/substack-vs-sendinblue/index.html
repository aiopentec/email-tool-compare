Substack vs Sendinblue: Detailed Comparison

Substack and Sendinblue serve very different needs in the email marketing space. Substack is built for writers and creators monetizing direct subscriber relationships, while Sendinblue (now Brevo) excels as an all-in-one CRM with transactional email and SMS capabilities. Understanding their core differences will help you choose the right platform for your specific use case.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Substack better than Sendinblue?

Neither is universally better—they serve different purposes. Substack excels for writers monetizing content, while Sendinblue is superior for businesses needing transactional email, SMS, and CRM features. Choose based on your primary use case.

Which is cheaper, Substack or Sendinblue?

Substack is cheaper if you have no paid subscribers (completely free). However, Substack takes 10% revenue on paid subscriptions. Sendinblue's paid plans start at $25/month but have no revenue share, making it more cost-effective for high-revenue newsletters.

Can I switch from Sendinblue to Substack?

Yes, you can export your contact list from Sendinblue and import it into Substack. However, note that Substack is designed primarily for newsletters, not general email marketing, so some features may not transfer directly.
