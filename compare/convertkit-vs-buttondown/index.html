ConvertKit vs Buttondown: Detailed Comparison for Email Marketing

ConvertKit and Buttondown are both freemium email marketing platforms, but they serve different audiences. ConvertKit targets creators and course sellers with built-in commerce and automation, while Buttondown appeals to developers and technical writers who prefer markdown editing. Choosing between them depends on your writing style, audience size, and feature requirements.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is ConvertKit better than Buttondown?

Neither is objectively better—it depends on your needs. ConvertKit excels for creators needing commerce and visual tools, while Buttondown is superior for developers valuing markdown and API access. Buttondown has higher customer satisfaction ratings (4.8/5 on G2), but ConvertKit offers more advanced marketing features.

Which is cheaper, ConvertKit or Buttondown?

Buttondown is significantly cheaper, starting at just $9/month compared to ConvertKit's $29/month. However, ConvertKit's free tier supports up to 1,000 subscribers versus Buttondown's 100, which may offset costs for growing creators.

Can I switch from Buttondown to ConvertKit?

Yes, both platforms support import and export functionality. Buttondown explicitly lists import/export as a feature, making migration straightforward. ConvertKit also accepts subscriber imports, though you may need to reconfigure automations and sequences after migration.
