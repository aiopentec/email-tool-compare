ConvertKit vs Buttondown: Detailed Comparison for 2025

ConvertKit targets creators and course sellers with advanced automation and commerce features, while Buttondown appeals to developers and technical writers who prefer markdown-based writing. Frequently asked questions

Is ConvertKit better than Buttondown?

It depends on your needs. ConvertKit is better for creators with advanced requirements like commerce, visual automations, and landing pages. Buttondown is better if you're a developer or technical writer who values simplicity, markdown support, and lower pricing. Both are excellent platforms with different strengths.

Which is cheaper, ConvertKit or Buttondown?

Buttondown is significantly cheaper, starting at $9/month compared to ConvertKit's $29/month. Buttondown also offers a more generous free tier with 100 subscribers versus ConvertKit's 1,000, though ConvertKit's higher limit may offset the cost difference for growing newsletters.

Can I switch from Buttondown to ConvertKit?

Yes, both platforms support import and export functionality, making migration possible. Buttondown explicitly lists import/export as a key feature. We recommend exporting your subscriber list and content from Buttondown, then importing it into ConvertKit. Always back up your data before switching platforms.
