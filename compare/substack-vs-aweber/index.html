Substack excels at monetizing newsletters directly from readers, while AWeber offers traditional email marketing automation for bloggers and solopreneurs. Frequently asked questions

Is Substack better than AWeber?

Neither is universally 'better'—it depends on your goal. Substack is superior for monetizing newsletters through paid subscriptions, while AWeber excels at email automation and marketing features. Choose based on whether you prioritize direct reader monetization or traditional email marketing.

Which is cheaper, Substack or AWeber?

Substack has a free tier with unlimited subscribers and no upfront costs, making it cheaper for starting out. AWeber's free plan supports only 500 subscribers. However, AWeber's paid plans start at $15/month with fixed pricing, while Substack takes a 10% revenue share if you offer paid subscriptions.

Can I switch from AWeber to Substack?

Yes, you can migrate your email list from AWeber to Substack, though the process is manual. AWeber allows you to export your subscriber list, which you can then import into Substack. Note that Substack is designed differently—it's more of a publishing platform than a traditional marketing automation tool, so workflows won't transfer directly.
