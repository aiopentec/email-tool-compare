ConvertKit vs Constant Contact: A Detailed Comparison

ConvertKit and Constant Contact serve different audiences in the email marketing space. ConvertKit is purpose-built for creators and course sellers with creator-first features, while Constant Contact focuses on small businesses and nonprofits seeking simplicity. Choosing between them depends on your business model and specific feature needs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is ConvertKit better than Constant Contact?

It depends on your use case. ConvertKit is better for creators and course sellers with superior automation and landing pages, while Constant Contact is better for nonprofits and local businesses seeking affordability and SMS capabilities. ConvertKit rates higher on G2 (4.4 vs 4.0) and offers a free tier, making it more accessible for startups.

Which is cheaper, ConvertKit or Constant Contact?

Constant Contact is cheaper at the entry point, starting at $12/month with no free tier. ConvertKit's paid plans start at $29/month, but it offers a free plan for up to 1,000 subscribers, which can be cost-effective for growing creators who don't need to pay immediately.

Can I switch from Constant Contact to ConvertKit?

Yes, both platforms support standard email list imports and exports. Most data including subscribers and basic information can be migrated, though you may need to reconfigure automations and templates. Both offer customer support to assist with the migration process.
