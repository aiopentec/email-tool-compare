ConvertKit focuses on creators and content-driven businesses, while Constant Contact targets nonprofits and small local businesses. Frequently Asked Questions

Is ConvertKit better than Constant Contact?

It depends on your needs. ConvertKit is better for creators and content-driven businesses with its visual automations and landing pages, while Constant Contact excels for nonprofits and local businesses needing event management and SMS. ConvertKit has higher user ratings overall (4.4 vs 4.0 on G2).

Which is cheaper, ConvertKit or Constant Contact?

ConvertKit is cheaper for small operations with its free tier supporting up to 1,000 subscribers at no cost. However, Constant Contact starts at $12/month with no free tier, making it more affordable per feature once you need paid plans. Your choice depends on your subscriber count and budget.

Can I switch from Constant Contact to ConvertKit?

Yes, both platforms support email list imports, so you can migrate your subscribers. ConvertKit has documented import processes and customer support to help with transitions. You'll want to map your tags and segments carefully during the migration to maintain proper segmentation.
