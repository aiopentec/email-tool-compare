Mailchimp vs ConvertKit: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins?

Mailchimp and ConvertKit are both popular email marketing platforms, but they cater to different audiences and use cases. Mailchimp targets small businesses and beginners seeking an all-in-one marketing solution, while ConvertKit focuses on creators, bloggers, and course sellers who need specialized automation. Choosing between them depends on your business model, budget, and specific feature requirements.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Mailchimp better than ConvertKit?

Neither is objectively 'better'—it depends on your needs. Mailchimp excels for budget-conscious beginners and small businesses, while ConvertKit is superior for creators and course sellers. ConvertKit has higher customer satisfaction ratings on Trustpilot (3.2 vs 1.5), but Mailchimp offers better pricing and a gentler learning curve for newcomers.

Which is cheaper, Mailchimp or ConvertKit?

Mailchimp is significantly cheaper, starting at $13/month compared to ConvertKit's $29/month. Additionally, Mailchimp's free tier supports 500 subscribers versus ConvertKit's 1,000, though ConvertKit's free plan offers more advanced creator-focused features despite the higher cost of paid plans.

Can I switch from ConvertKit to Mailchimp?

Yes, you can migrate your subscriber list from ConvertKit to Mailchimp. Both platforms support CSV exports and imports, making the transition straightforward. However, automation workflows and sequences won't transfer automatically, so you'll need to recreate those manually in your new platform.
