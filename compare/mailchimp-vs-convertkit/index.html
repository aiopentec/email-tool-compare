Mailchimp targets small businesses and beginners with affordable pricing and broad functionality, while ConvertKit focuses on creators, bloggers, and course sellers with specialized features. Frequently Asked Questions

Is Mailchimp better than ConvertKit?

Neither is universally better—it depends on your use case. Mailchimp excels for beginners and small businesses due to lower pricing and accessibility, while ConvertKit is superior for creators and course sellers thanks to its specialized features and commerce capabilities. ConvertKit has better customer reviews on Trustpilot (3.2 vs 1.5), suggesting stronger user satisfaction overall.

Which is cheaper, Mailchimp or ConvertKit?

Mailchimp is significantly cheaper, starting at $13/month compared to ConvertKit's $29/month. Mailchimp's free tier also accommodates 500 subscribers versus ConvertKit's 1,000, though both offer solid freemium options for getting started.

Can I switch from ConvertKit to Mailchimp?

Yes, both platforms allow you to export your subscriber lists and migrate to the other service. Most email marketing platforms support CSV exports, making the transition straightforward. However, you'll need to rebuild automations and sequences manually, as they don't transfer between platforms.
