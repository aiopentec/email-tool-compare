Substack vs Campaign Monitor: Head-to-Head Comparison

Substack and Campaign Monitor serve fundamentally different email marketing needs. Substack is designed for independent writers and creators looking to monetize newsletters directly, while Campaign Monitor caters to agencies and businesses managing professional email campaigns. Understanding their core differences will help you choose the right platform for your goals.

Choose Substack if...

  • You're an independent writer or creator wanting to monetize content through paid subscriptions with zero upfront costs
  • You want a built-in audience discovery feature and don't need advanced segmentation or multi-brand management

Choose Campaign Monitor if...

  • You're an agency or business managing multiple client brands from one dashboard

  • You prefer a transparent, predictable monthly pricing model over revenue-sharing arrangements

Frequently asked questions

Is Substack better than Campaign Monitor?

Neither is universally 'better'—they serve different purposes. Substack excels for individual creators monetizing newsletters with a built-in audience, while Campaign Monitor is superior for agencies and businesses needing professional email marketing infrastructure. Choose based on your use case, not reputation.

Which is cheaper, Substack or Campaign Monitor?

Substack is cheaper for most users since it's free to start and only takes a 10% commission on paid subscriptions you actually make. Campaign Monitor requires a minimum $11/month subscription regardless of email volume, making it costlier for beginners but more predictable for established businesses.

Can I switch from Campaign Monitor to Substack?

You can export your subscriber lists from Campaign Monitor and import them into Substack, but the platforms serve different purposes. Substack focuses on paid newsletter monetization, while Campaign Monitor targets marketing campaigns, so migration depends on whether Substack's model aligns with your goals.
