Campaign Monitor vs Omnisend: Complete Comparison

Campaign Monitor and Omnisend are both established email marketing platforms, but they serve different audiences. Campaign Monitor specializes in multi-brand management for agencies, while Omnisend focuses on omnichannel marketing for e-commerce businesses. Your choice depends on your business model, budget, and whether you need SMS and push notification capabilities.

Which Should You Choose?

Choose Omnisend if…

  • You manage multiple brands and need consolidated campaign management across clients
  • You're an agency requiring professional template builders and white-label options
  • You primarily need email marketing without SMS or push notification requirements

Choose Campaign Monitor if…

  • You run an e-commerce business and want email combined with SMS marketing
  • You're just starting out and want to test the platform with a free tier
  • You need pre-built automation workflows and advanced segmentation for online stores

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Campaign Monitor better than Omnisend?

It depends on your needs. Omnisend scores higher overall (4.5 vs 4.1 on G2) and is better for e-commerce with omnichannel features. Campaign Monitor excels for agencies managing multiple brands. For most businesses, Omnisend offers more modern features at a better price point.

Which is cheaper, Campaign Monitor or Omnisend?

Omnisend is significantly cheaper for small businesses, offering a free tier for up to 250 subscribers before charging $16/month. Campaign Monitor has no free tier and starts at $11/month, making Omnisend more budget-friendly for startups. For larger lists, pricing depends on your subscriber count with each platform.

Can I switch from Omnisend to Campaign Monitor?

Yes, both platforms support standard email list imports and exports. Most email marketing platforms allow you to export your contacts in CSV format. However, you may lose some custom segmentation data and automation workflows, so plan your migration carefully.
