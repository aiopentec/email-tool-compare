Brevo vs Substack: Detailed Comparison

Brevo and Substack serve fundamentally different purposes within the email marketing space. Brevo is an all-in-one CRM and marketing automation platform ideal for businesses managing customer relationships, while Substack is built for individual creators and writers to build and monetize newsletters directly. Understanding their core differences is essential for choosing the right platform for your specific use case.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Brevo better than Substack?

It depends on your use case. Brevo is superior for businesses needing comprehensive marketing automation, CRM, and multi-channel capabilities. Substack excels for individual creators wanting to monetize newsletters with minimal setup. They're designed for different audiences rather than being direct competitors.

Which is cheaper, Brevo or Substack?

Substack is free to use with unlimited subscribers, though you pay 10% commission on paid subscriptions. Brevo offers a generous free tier for up to 100,000 contacts but requires paid plans starting at $25/month for advanced features. The cost-effectiveness depends on your subscriber count and monetization strategy.

Can I switch from Substack to Brevo?

Yes, you can export your subscriber list from Substack and import it into Brevo. However, you'll lose Substack-specific features like paid subscriptions and their built-in audience discovery. The transition is straightforward for basic email lists but requires rebuilding any monetization infrastructure.
