GetResponse vs Campaign Monitor: Complete Comparison

GetResponse and Campaign Monitor are both established email marketing platforms, but they serve different audiences. GetResponse is an all-in-one solution ideal for course creators and webinar hosts, while Campaign Monitor specializes in beautiful email design and multi-brand management for agencies. Your choice depends on whether you need advanced automation and courses or focused design and team collaboration.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is GetResponse better than Campaign Monitor?

Neither is universally better—it depends on your needs. GetResponse excels for creators needing webinars, courses, and automation, while Campaign Monitor is superior for agencies managing multiple brands with a focus on design. GetResponse has better ratings (4.2 vs 4.1 on G2) and offers a free tier, making it more accessible for startups.

Which is cheaper, GetResponse or Campaign Monitor?

Campaign Monitor starts at $11/month versus GetResponse's $19/month for paid plans. However, GetResponse offers a free tier supporting up to 500 subscribers, which can eliminate costs entirely for small operations. Campaign Monitor has no free option but provides more affordable entry for those ready to pay.

Can I switch from Campaign Monitor to GetResponse?

Yes, both platforms support standard email list exports and imports. Most email marketing tools allow you to export your contacts and segments, which you can then import into GetResponse. Campaign Monitor also provides migration support to help transfer your data, though you'll need to recreate custom workflows in GetResponse's automation builder.
