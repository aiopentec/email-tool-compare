Choosing between them depends on your business model, team structure, and specific feature needs.

Which Should You Choose?

Choose Campaign Monitor if…

  • You're a creator, blogger, or course seller who needs built-in landing pages and commerce features
  • You want to start for free with up to 1,000 subscribers before committing to paid plans
  • You need visual automation builders and subscriber tagging for managing audience segments

Choose ConvertKit if…

  • You're an agency or team managing multiple client brands and need multi-brand management
  • You need transactional email capabilities alongside marketing automation
  • You prefer a lower entry price point starting at just $11/month

Frequently Asked Questions

Is ConvertKit better than Campaign Monitor?

It depends on your use case. ConvertKit is better for individual creators and course sellers due to its specialized features like landing pages and commerce integration. Campaign Monitor excels for agencies managing multiple brands. ConvertKit has higher user satisfaction ratings (4.4 vs 4.1 on G2), making it the better choice for most creators.

Which is cheaper, ConvertKit or Campaign Monitor?

Campaign Monitor starts at $11/month, making it cheaper for paid plans. However, ConvertKit offers a free tier supporting up to 1,000 subscribers, making it the more affordable option if you're just starting out. For growing businesses, Campaign Monitor's lower entry price may provide better value at scale.

Can I switch from Campaign Monitor to ConvertKit?

Yes, both platforms support email list imports and exports, making migration straightforward. Most users can export their subscriber data from Campaign Monitor and import it into ConvertKit without losing contact information. However, you'll need to manually recreate automation sequences and campaigns in your new platform.
