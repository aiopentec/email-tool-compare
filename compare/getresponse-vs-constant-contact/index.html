GetResponse vs Constant Contact: Complete Comparison

GetResponse and Constant Contact are both established email marketing platforms, but they serve different business needs. GetResponse excels for creators and course sellers with webinar and funnel-building tools, while Constant Contact focuses on simplicity for local businesses and nonprofits. This guide breaks down their key differences to help you choose the right platform.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is GetResponse better than Constant Contact?

It depends on your use case. GetResponse is better for content creators, course sellers, and webinar hosts who need advanced automation and funnel-building. Constant Contact is better for local businesses and nonprofits that prioritize simplicity, SMS marketing, and e-commerce features. Both are solid platforms with comparable ratings.

Which is cheaper, GetResponse or Constant Contact?

Constant Contact starts at $12/month, while GetResponse starts at $19/month for paid plans. However, GetResponse offers a free tier supporting 500 subscribers with full feature access, making it the better value if you're starting out. Constant Contact has no free option but may be cheaper for very small lists.

Can I switch from Constant Contact to GetResponse?

Yes, both platforms support email list imports and exports. Most email marketing tools allow you to export your contacts as a CSV file and import them into GetResponse. The transition is straightforward, though you may need to rebuild email sequences and automation workflows in the new platform.
