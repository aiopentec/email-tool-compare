Substack vs Drip: Detailed Comparison for 2025

Substack and Drip serve fundamentally different purposes in the email marketing landscape. Substack is designed for writers and creators to build paid newsletters directly, while Drip specializes in e-commerce email automation and revenue tracking. Your choice depends on whether you're monetizing content or running an online store.

Frequently asked questions

Is Substack better than Drip?

Neither is universally better—they serve different markets. Substack excels for individual creators monetizing newsletters, while Drip is superior for e-commerce businesses needing automated product-based marketing. Your choice depends entirely on your business model and goals.

Which is cheaper, Substack or Drip?

Substack is free to start with no upfront costs; you only pay 10% commission on paid subscriptions you generate. Drip requires a minimum $39/month investment. For bootstrapped creators, Substack's free tier is significantly cheaper initially.

Can I switch from Drip to Substack?

Yes, you can export your subscriber list from Drip and import it to Substack, though some platform-specific features (like e-commerce tracking) won't transfer. The switch is straightforward for basic email lists, but you'll lose Drip's automation and e-commerce functionality.
