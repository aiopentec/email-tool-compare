GetResponse vs Campaign Monitor: Detailed Comparison

GetResponse and Campaign Monitor are both established email marketing platforms, but they serve different audiences and use cases. GetResponse is a freemium all-in-one platform ideal for solopreneurs and course creators, while Campaign Monitor specializes in beautiful templates and multi-brand management for agencies. This guide breaks down their key differences to help you choose the right tool.

Frequently asked questions

Is GetResponse better than Campaign Monitor?

It depends on your needs. GetResponse is better for solopreneurs and creators due to its free tier, webinar features, and all-in-one platform. Campaign Monitor is better for agencies managing multiple brands with a focus on design. GetResponse scores slightly higher on reviews (4.2 vs 4.1 on G2).

Which is cheaper, GetResponse or Campaign Monitor?

GetResponse offers a free tier up to 500 subscribers, making it cheaper for beginners. Campaign Monitor starts at $11/month with no free option. However, Campaign Monitor's paid plans are competitively priced for agencies needing multi-brand management.

Can I switch from Campaign Monitor to GetResponse?

Yes, both platforms allow you to export your contact lists and campaign data. GetResponse provides import tools to help migrate your subscribers and previous campaigns. Most migration can be completed within a few hours, though you may need to recreate some automation workflows manually.
