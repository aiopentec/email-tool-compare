GetResponse vs AWeber: Complete Comparison

Both GetResponse and AWeber are established email marketing platforms founded in 1998, offering freemium models with similar free tier limits. The key difference lies in their focus: GetResponse positions itself as a comprehensive marketing suite with webinars and courses, while AWeber emphasizes simplicity for bloggers and solopreneurs. Your choice depends on whether you need advanced features or straightforward email automation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is GetResponse better than AWeber?

Neither is objectively 'better'—it depends on your needs. GetResponse excels for creators needing webinars, courses, and funnels, while AWeber is better for those wanting simplicity and affordability. GetResponse has a higher G2 rating (4.2 vs 4.2 tie), but AWeber scores higher on Trustpilot (4.0 vs 3.7), suggesting better customer satisfaction.

Which is cheaper, GetResponse or AWeber?

AWeber is slightly cheaper at $15/month vs GetResponse's $19/month for paid plans. Both offer identical free tiers with 500 contact limits. However, GetResponse's additional features (webinars, courses) may provide better value depending on your business model.

Can I switch from AWeber to GetResponse?

Yes, both platforms support contact list imports and CSV exports, making migration straightforward. Most users can transition their subscribers and email sequences within a few hours. GetResponse's automation builder may require rebuilding some complex workflows, but basic lists transfer seamlessly.
