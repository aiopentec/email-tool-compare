ConvertKit vs AWeber: Complete Comparison

Both ConvertKit and AWeber are established email marketing platforms offering free tiers and creator-friendly features, but they serve different audiences and strengths. ConvertKit targets content creators and course sellers with visual automation and commerce tools, while AWeber prioritizes simplicity for bloggers and solopreneurs. This guide breaks down their key differences to help you choose the right platform for your business.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is ConvertKit better than AWeber?

It depends on your needs. ConvertKit excels for creators and course sellers with advanced automation and commerce features, earning a higher G2 rating (4.4 vs 4.2). However, AWeber scores better on Trustpilot (4.0 vs 3.2) and offers greater simplicity for beginners. ConvertKit is the better overall choice if you need modern features; AWeber wins if you prioritize ease of use and cost.

Which is cheaper, ConvertKit or AWeber?

AWeber is more affordable, starting at $15/month compared to ConvertKit's $29/month. Additionally, AWeber's free tier supports 500 subscribers while ConvertKit allows 1,000. However, both offer free plans, making them accessible entry points. Your actual costs will depend on subscriber growth and required feature complexity.

Can I switch from AWeber to ConvertKit?

Yes, both platforms allow you to export your subscriber lists, though the process varies. Most users can export contacts as CSV files and import them into ConvertKit. It's recommended to back up your automations and sequences before switching, as migration doesn't transfer workflows automatically. Both platforms offer customer support to help with the transition if needed.
