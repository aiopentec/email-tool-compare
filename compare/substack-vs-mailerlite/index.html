MailerLite vs Substack: Detailed Comparison

MailerLite and Substack are both popular email marketing platforms, but they serve different audiences and purposes. MailerLite is a traditional email marketing tool designed for businesses building customer relationships, while Substack focuses on writers and creators monetizing their content directly. Understanding their core differences will help you choose the right platform for your goals.

Which Should You Choose?

Choose Substack if...

  • You're running a small business or ecommerce store and need marketing automation
  • You want advanced features like landing pages, popups, and surveys out of the box
  • You prefer transparent subscription pricing over revenue-share models
  • You need integrations with tools like Shopify, WooCommerce, or other business platforms

Choose MailerLite if...

  • You're a writer or creator looking to monetize content directly from subscribers
  • You want unlimited subscribers on a free plan with no subscriber limits
  • You prefer a revenue-share model where Substack takes a percentage of paid subscriptions only
  • You want built-in audience discovery through Substack's creator network
  • You're launching a paid newsletter and want Substack's native monetization features

Frequently Asked Questions

Is MailerLite better than Substack?

It depends on your use case. MailerLite is better for businesses, ecommerce stores, and marketers who need automation and advanced features. Substack is better for writers and creators monetizing content directly. MailerLite scores higher on review platforms and offers more traditional marketing tools.

Which is cheaper, MailerLite or Substack?

Substack is technically free forever—it only takes 10% when you earn money from paid subscriptions. MailerLite offers free plans up to 1,000 subscribers, then starts at $9/month. For most small businesses, MailerLite's fixed pricing is more predictable than Substack's revenue-share model.

Can I switch from Substack to MailerLite?

Yes, you can export your subscriber list from Substack and import it into MailerLite. However, you'll lose Substack-specific features like paid subscriptions and the creator network. The switch is straightforward for the email list itself, but you'll need to rebuild any monetization strategy.

Does MailerLite have paid subscriptions like Substack?

No, MailerLite doesn't have native paid subscription features. It's designed for traditional email marketing campaigns. If you want to monetize subscriber content, you'd need to use a separate payment processor or choose Substack instead.

Can Substack handle ecommerce marketing?

Substack lacks ecommerce integrations and automation features needed for serious marketing campaigns. While you can email your subscribers, it's not designed for abandoned cart emails, segmentation, or Shopify integration. MailerLite is the better choice for ecommerce businesses.
