MailerLite vs Substack: Detailed Comparison for 2025

MailerLite and Substack are both popular email platforms, but they serve different audiences and use cases. MailerLite is a comprehensive email marketing suite designed for businesses, while Substack is built specifically for writers and creators who want to monetize newsletters directly. Understanding their differences will help you choose the right platform for your goals.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is MailerLite better than Substack?

It depends on your use case. MailerLite is better for businesses needing comprehensive marketing automation, landing pages, and e-commerce features. Substack is better for individual creators focused on monetizing content directly. MailerLite has higher ratings (4.6 vs 4.0 on G2) and is more versatile for business needs.

Which is cheaper, MailerLite or Substack?

Substack has no upfront costs and only takes 10% of paid subscription revenue, making it ideal if you have few or no paid subscribers. MailerLite offers a free tier for up to 1,000 subscribers, then starts at $9/month. For most businesses with growing lists, MailerLite's fixed pricing is more predictable than Substack's revenue share.

Can I switch from Substack to MailerLite?

Yes, you can export your subscriber list from Substack and import it into MailerLite. However, you'll need to rebuild your email campaigns and automation. The transition is straightforward from a technical standpoint, though you'll lose Substack's built-in monetization features.
