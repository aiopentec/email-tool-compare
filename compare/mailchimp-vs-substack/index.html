Mailchimp vs Substack: Detailed Comparison

Mailchimp and Substack are both popular email marketing platforms, but they serve very different purposes. Mailchimp is a comprehensive marketing automation suite ideal for businesses, while Substack is built for individual writers and creators to monetize newsletters directly. Understanding their core differences will help you choose the right tool for your specific use case.

Which Should You Choose?

Choose Mailchimp if...

  • You're running a business and need marketing automation and advanced analytics
  • You want a traditional email marketing platform with professional features like A/B testing and audience segmentation
  • You plan to send campaigns to more than 500 subscribers and prefer transparent, predictable pricing

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Mailchimp better than Substack?

It depends on your use case. Mailchimp is better for businesses needing marketing automation and advanced features, while Substack excels for individual creators monetizing newsletters. Mailchimp has higher overall ratings and more comprehensive tools, but Substack offers unique monetization features and unlimited free subscribers.

Which is cheaper, Mailchimp or Substack?

Substack is cheaper if you're monetizing through paid subscriptions, as it takes only a commission without upfront costs. Mailchimp is cheaper for traditional email campaigns but charges per subscriber once you exceed 500. Choose based on whether you plan to charge subscribers.

Can I switch from Substack to Mailchimp?

Yes, you can export your subscriber list from Substack and import it into Mailchimp. However, note that switching means losing Substack's built-in monetization features and audience discovery, so consider whether Mailchimp's tools justify the move before migrating.
