Substack vs Constant Contact: A Complete Comparison

Substack and Constant Contact serve different segments of the email marketing landscape. Substack is designed for individual writers and creators monetizing direct relationships with subscribers, while Constant Contact caters to small businesses, nonprofits, and organizations needing comprehensive marketing tools. Understanding their distinct approaches will help you choose the right platform for your goals.

Frequently asked questions

Is Substack better than Constant Contact?

Neither is objectively better—it depends on your use case. Substack excels for individual creators monetizing content, while Constant Contact is superior for businesses needing integrated marketing tools like SMS, social ads, and e-commerce. Constant Contact offers more comprehensive business features, whereas Substack provides a simpler, creator-focused experience.

Which is cheaper, Substack or Constant Contact?

Substack is cheaper if you're starting out—it's completely free with unlimited subscribers. Constant Contact requires payment starting at $12/month. However, Substack takes a revenue cut from paid subscriptions, so costs may increase as you monetize; Constant Contact's fixed pricing may be more economical for established businesses with large subscriber bases.

Can I switch from Constant Contact to Substack?

Yes, you can export your email list from Constant Contact and import it into Substack. However, the platforms serve different purposes—Substack is optimized for monetized newsletters while Constant Contact handles broader marketing campaigns. The switch is technically possible but may require restructuring your content and strategy to align with Substack's creator-focused approach.
