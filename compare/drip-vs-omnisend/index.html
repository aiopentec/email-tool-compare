Frequently asked questions

Is Drip better than Omnisend?

It depends on your priorities. Drip excels in revenue attribution and deep ecommerce integration, making it ideal for stores needing advanced tracking. Omnisend offers better value with a free tier, omnichannel capabilities, and higher customer satisfaction ratings. For most growing ecommerce businesses, Omnisend provides more features at a lower entry price.

Which is cheaper, Drip or Omnisend?

Omnisend is significantly cheaper for startups, offering a free plan for up to 250 subscribers with no credit card required. Drip starts at $39/month with no free option. Even Omnisend's paid plans begin at $16/month, making it more accessible for budget-conscious businesses testing the platform.

Can I switch from Omnisend to Drip?

Yes, both platforms support standard email list exports and migration tools. You'll want to export your contacts, segments, and automation workflows before switching. Both companies typically provide migration support documentation, though you may need to manually recreate complex workflows depending on feature differences.
