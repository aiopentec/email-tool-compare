Mailchimp emphasizes ease of use and affordability for beginners, while Campaign Monitor targets agencies and teams managing multiple brands. Frequently asked questions

Is Mailchimp better than Campaign Monitor?

It depends on your needs. Mailchimp excels for beginners and small businesses due to its free tier and user-friendly interface, with a higher G2 rating of 4.3. Campaign Monitor is better suited for agencies and teams managing multiple brands, offering advanced features like journey designer and multi-brand management. Choose based on your use case rather than overall superiority.

Which is cheaper, Mailchimp or Campaign Monitor?

Mailchimp is significantly cheaper for small operations, offering a free tier for up to 500 subscribers with no time limit. Campaign Monitor requires payment from the start at $11/month. However, as your subscriber list grows, pricing will eventually depend on your specific volume and feature needs.

Can I switch from Campaign Monitor to Mailchimp?

Yes, both platforms typically support email list imports, making migration possible. Most email marketing tools allow you to export your subscriber lists and import them into Mailchimp. You'll need to recreate your campaigns and automation workflows, but your core contact data can transfer smoothly.
