Substack is built for individual writers and creators monetizing their audience directly, while Moosend targets small businesses seeking robust email automation and marketing features. Understanding their strengths will help you choose the right platform for your specific needs.

Frequently asked questions

Is Substack better than Moosend?

Neither is objectively 'better'—they serve different purposes. Substack excels at creator monetization and paid newsletters, while Moosend is superior for email marketing automation and small business campaigns. Choose based on your primary goal: direct reader revenue or marketing automation.

Which is cheaper, Substack or Moosend?

Substack is free forever with no subscription fees, though it takes a 10% cut of paid subscription revenue. Moosend's free tier covers 1,000 subscribers with unlimited emails, and paid plans start at $9/month. For small businesses, Moosend is typically cheaper; for creators with high subscription revenue, Substack's cut may exceed Moosend's fees.

Can I switch from Moosend to Substack?

Yes, you can export your subscriber list from Moosend and import it into Substack. However, note that Substack is designed for creators monetizing newsletters, not general marketing, so the platform experience and feature set are quite different. Plan your transition carefully based on your actual use case.
