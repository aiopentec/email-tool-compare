Buttondown vs Constant Contact: Detailed Comparison

Buttondown and Constant Contact are two established email marketing platforms with distinctly different approaches. Buttondown caters to developers and technical writers seeking simplicity and markdown support, while Constant Contact targets small businesses and nonprofits needing comprehensive marketing tools. Understanding their differences will help you choose the right platform for your specific needs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Buttondown better than Constant Contact?

It depends on your use case. Buttondown excels for developers and technical writers with markdown-first publishing and a free tier, earning a 4.8 G2 rating. Constant Contact is better for general small businesses needing comprehensive marketing features like e-commerce and SMS, though it has lower satisfaction ratings (4.0 G2, 2.8 Trustpilot).

Which is cheaper, Buttondown or Constant Contact?

Buttondown is cheaper overall. It offers a free plan for up to 100 subscribers with no credit card required, then starts at $9/month. Constant Contact has no free tier and begins at $12/month. For budget-conscious users or those just starting out, Buttondown provides better value.

Can I switch from Constant Contact to Buttondown?

Yes, both platforms support import/export functionality. Buttondown specifically lists import/export as a key feature, making it straightforward to migrate your subscriber lists and past campaigns from Constant Contact to Buttondown.
