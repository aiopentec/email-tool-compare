GetResponse vs Substack: Complete Comparison

GetResponse and Substack are both email marketing platforms, but they serve different audiences and use different business models. GetResponse is a comprehensive marketing suite ideal for businesses running courses and webinars, while Substack is designed for individual writers and creators monetizing through paid subscriptions. Understanding their key differences will help you choose the right tool for your specific goals.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is GetResponse better than Substack?

It depends on your use case. GetResponse is better for businesses needing comprehensive marketing tools like webinars, courses, and automation. Substack excels for individual creators monetizing through direct paid subscriptions. GetResponse has higher ratings on review sites (G2: 4.2 vs 4.0), suggesting broader business appeal.

Which is cheaper, GetResponse or Substack?

Substack is free forever with no subscriber limits, making it cheaper for startups. However, Substack takes 10% of revenue from paid subscriptions, while GetResponse charges fixed monthly fees. For large subscriber bases generating significant revenue, GetResponse's flat fees may be more economical.

Can I switch from Substack to GetResponse?

Yes, most email platforms allow you to export your subscriber list from Substack and import it into GetResponse. However, you'll lose Substack's built-in audience discovery features and monetization infrastructure. Both platforms support CSV exports, making migration straightforward.
