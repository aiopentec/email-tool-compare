Klaviyo vs Substack: Detailed Comparison

Klaviyo and Substack serve fundamentally different email marketing needs. Klaviyo powers e-commerce marketing automation for Shopify and WooCommerce stores, while Substack enables writers to build monetized newsletters with direct subscriber relationships. Your choice depends on whether you're optimizing sales or building a subscription-based publishing business.

Which Should You Choose?

Choose Klaviyo if...

  • You run an e-commerce store on Shopify or WooCommerce and need advanced marketing automation
  • You want SMS marketing, predictive analytics, and sophisticated customer segmentation
  • You need professional-grade features with dedicated integrations for online sales

Choose Substack if...

  • You're a writer or creator looking to monetize your audience through paid subscriptions
  • You prefer a platform with unlimited free subscribers and no upfront costs
  • You want built-in podcast hosting, recommendations, and a discovery network for your content

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Klaviyo better than Substack?

Not necessarily—it depends on your goals. Klaviyo excels at e-commerce marketing automation with superior features and ratings (4.6 G2 vs 4.0), making it better for stores. Substack is superior for creators monetizing newsletters. They're designed for different purposes rather than direct competition.

Which is cheaper, Klaviyo or Substack?

Substack is cheaper for creators with unlimited free subscribers and a revenue-share model (no upfront cost). Klaviyo's free tier caps at 500 contacts with paid plans starting at $45/month. For scaling, Substack's approach is more cost-effective if you're monetizing subscriptions.

Can I switch from Substack to Klaviyo?

Yes, you can export your subscriber list from Substack and import it into Klaviyo. However, they serve different purposes—Substack handles monetization while Klaviyo handles e-commerce marketing. Many creators use both platforms simultaneously for different functions.
