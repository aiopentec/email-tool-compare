Klaviyo vs Substack: Complete Comparison Guide

Klaviyo and Substack serve different audiences within email marketing. Klaviyo is built for e-commerce businesses automating customer communications, while Substack is designed for individual creators and writers monetizing newsletters directly. Understanding their core differences will help you choose the right platform for your goals.

Which Should You Choose?

Choose Klaviyo if...

  • You run an e-commerce store and need advanced automation and segmentation
  • You want predictive analytics and SMS marketing integrated with email
  • You require native Shopify or WooCommerce integrations for your business

Choose Substack if...

  • You're an independent writer or creator wanting to monetize directly from readers
  • You prefer a simple, distraction-free publishing platform with no subscriber limits
  • You want access to Substack's built-in audience and discovery features

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Klaviyo better than Substack?

Neither is universally 'better'—they serve different purposes. Klaviyo excels for e-commerce businesses needing marketing automation, while Substack is superior for individual creators monetizing newsletters. Your choice depends on your business model and goals.

Which is cheaper, Klaviyo or Substack?

Substack is free to start with no upfront costs, though you pay 10% of paid subscription revenue. Klaviyo charges based on subscriber count ($45+/month starting at 500+ contacts). For small creator operations, Substack is typically cheaper; for e-commerce with high revenue, Klaviyo's ROI often justifies the cost.

Can I switch from Substack to Klaviyo?

Yes, both platforms allow you to export subscriber lists, though the process isn't automatic. You'll need to manually migrate your audience and rebuild automations in Klaviyo. This is most practical if you're scaling from creator to business model and need Klaviyo's advanced features.
