Frequently asked questions

Is GetResponse better than Drip?

It depends on your needs. GetResponse is superior for businesses requiring webinars, courses, and multi-channel marketing, while Drip excels for e-commerce companies needing specialized store integration and revenue tracking. GetResponse has a higher overall feature set, while Drip achieves a slightly higher G2 rating (4.4 vs 4.2).

Which is cheaper, GetResponse or Drip?

GetResponse is significantly cheaper to start, offering a free plan for up to 500 subscribers with no credit card required. Drip has no free tier and starts at $39/month. However, GetResponse's paid plans begin at $19/month, making it the more budget-friendly option overall.

Can I switch from Drip to GetResponse?

Yes, both platforms support email list imports and exports, making migration relatively straightforward. You'll need to export your contacts from Drip and import them into GetResponse, though automation workflows and campaign settings will need to be recreated manually in most cases.
