Substack vs Omnisend: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins?

Substack and Omnisend serve fundamentally different email marketing needs. Substack is built for writers and creators to monetize subscriber relationships, while Omnisend is purpose-built for ecommerce businesses managing multi-channel campaigns. Choosing between them depends entirely on your business model and primary use case.

Frequently asked questions

Is Substack better than Omnisend?

Neither is objectively better—they serve different purposes. Substack wins for writers monetizing content directly, while Omnisend excels for ecommerce businesses. Your choice depends on whether you're building a newsletter business or running an online store.

Which is cheaper, Substack or Omnisend?

Substack is completely free unless you enable paid subscriptions (taking 30% revenue share). Omnisend is free for up to 250 subscribers, then starts at $16/month. For large subscriber bases, Substack's unlimited free tier is more cost-effective.

Can I switch from Omnisend to Substack?

Yes, both platforms allow subscriber list exports via CSV. However, they're designed for different use cases, so switching makes sense only if your business model shifts from ecommerce to direct creator monetization. Substack lacks the SMS and automation features most ecommerce businesses rely on.
