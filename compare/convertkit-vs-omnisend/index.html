ConvertKit vs Omnisend: Detailed Comparison

ConvertKit and Omnisend are both freemium email marketing platforms, but they serve different audiences. ConvertKit focuses on creators and course sellers with landing pages and commerce features, while Omnisend caters to e-commerce brands with integrated SMS and push notifications. Choosing between them depends on your business model and whether you need multichannel capabilities.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is ConvertKit better than Omnisend?

Neither is universally better—it depends on your needs. ConvertKit excels for creators and bloggers with its landing pages and commerce tools, while Omnisend is superior for e-commerce brands seeking SMS and email integration. Omnisend has higher customer satisfaction ratings and lower pricing, but ConvertKit offers a larger free tier.

Which is cheaper, ConvertKit or Omnisend?

Omnisend is more affordable, starting at $16/month compared to ConvertKit's $29/month. However, ConvertKit's free tier accommodates 1,000 subscribers versus Omnisend's 250, making it better value if you're building an audience before paying.

Can I switch from Omnisend to ConvertKit?

Yes, both platforms support subscriber list exports via CSV files, making migration straightforward. However, you'll need to manually recreate automations and workflows as they don't transfer automatically. Plan the switch during a low-activity period to minimize disruption.
