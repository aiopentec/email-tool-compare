Frequently Asked Questions

Is ConvertKit better than GetResponse?

It depends on your needs. ConvertKit excels for creators with simpler workflows and e-commerce needs, offering an intuitive interface and strong community. GetResponse is better if you need webinars, online courses, and advanced automation all in one platform. ConvertKit scores slightly higher on G2 (4.4 vs 4.2), but GetResponse rates better on Trustpilot (3.7 vs 3.2).

Which is cheaper, ConvertKit or GetResponse?

GetResponse is more affordable overall, starting at $19/month compared to ConvertKit's $29/month. GetResponse also offers a higher free plan ($0 vs $0), though with fewer subscribers (500 vs 1,000). For budget-conscious users, GetResponse typically provides more value for the price, especially if you need multiple features like webinars and courses.

Can I switch from GetResponse to ConvertKit?

Yes, you can migrate your email list from GetResponse to ConvertKit. Both platforms support CSV imports and have documented migration processes. Most email marketing tools allow you to export your subscriber lists, though you'll need to manually recreate automations and sequences in the new platform. Always back up your data before switching.
