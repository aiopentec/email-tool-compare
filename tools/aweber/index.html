AWeber is email marketing made easy that positions itself for bloggers and solopreneurs wanting simplicity. Founded in 1998, it has become one of the most recognised platforms in the email marketing space. In this review we cover what it does well, where it falls short, and who should actually use it.

Our verdict: AWeber is a solid choice for bloggers and solopreneurs wanting simplicity. Its free tier is genuinely useful. The main trade-off is between its particular strengths and the limitations that make alternatives worth considering for some users.

Frequently asked questions

Is AWeber free?

Yes, AWeber has a free plan supporting up to 500 subscribers. This makes it accessible for new senders testing the platform.

What is AWeber best used for?

AWeber is best for bloggers and solopreneurs wanting simplicity. Its standout features include autoresponders, RSS-to-email, landing pages, which suit this use case well.

Is AWeber good for beginners?

AWeber is generally beginner-friendly. The interface is straightforward for basic campaigns, though advanced automation features have a learning curve.

Does AWeber have an affiliate program?

Yes, AWeber runs an affiliate program paying 30% recurring. This is worth considering if you plan to recommend the platform to your audience.
