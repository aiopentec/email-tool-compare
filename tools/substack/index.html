Substack Review: The Newsletter Platform for Direct Monetization

Revenue-Share Free plan available From $0/mo G2: 4.0★

Substack is a content creation platform that enables writers to build and monetize newsletters directly through paid subscriptions. Founded in 2017, it's designed specifically for creators who want to build an audience and generate revenue without relying on advertising or sponsorships. The platform is best suited for independent writers, journalists, and thought leaders looking to establish direct relationships with paying subscribers.

Our verdict: Substack excels as a creator-first platform with genuine monetization opportunities and an unlimited subscriber base, making it compelling for writers serious about building a sustainable income. However, the platform's lower Trustpilot rating (2.9) and lack of advanced marketing features suggest potential friction with customer support and limited tools for sophisticated audience growth strategies. Overall, it's an excellent choice for writers prioritizing simplicity and direct subscriber relationships over complex marketing automation.

Pros

  • Revenue-share model with no upfront fees—you only pay when earning money
  • Unlimited subscribers on all plans with no per-subscriber cost
  • Built-in audience discovery through the Substack notes feed and recommendations
  • Integrated podcast hosting and multimedia content support alongside email newsletters
  • Simple, writer-focused interface that requires minimal technical knowledge

Cons

  • Low Trustpilot rating (2.9) suggests potential customer support or reliability concerns
  • Limited advanced email marketing features compared to dedicated platforms like ConvertKit or Mailchimp
  • No affiliate program to incentivize promotion or partnership opportunities
  • Algorithm-dependent discovery means growth can be unpredictable for new creators

Pricing

Substack operates on a revenue-share model where the platform is completely free to use, and Substack takes a 10% cut only when you earn money from paid subscriptions. There are no subscriber limits or tiered pricing plans—all creators access the same features regardless of their earnings level.

Who is Substack best for?

Substack is ideal for independent writers, journalists, and subject matter experts who want to monetize directly from their audience without intermediaries or advertising. It's particularly valuable for creators already confident in their ability to build an audience, as the platform prioritizes simplicity and subscriber relationships over complex growth tools.

Key features

  • Paid Subscriptions
  • Podcast Hosting
  • Notes Feed
  • Recommendations
  • No Subscriber Limit
  • Built-In Audience

Frequently asked questions

Is Substack free?

Yes, Substack is completely free to start with no upfront costs. The platform uses a revenue-share model, taking a 10% commission only when you earn money through paid subscriptions. You can build and maintain a free newsletter indefinitely at no cost.

What is Substack best used for?

Substack is best used for publishing and monetizing newsletters directly to subscribers who pay for exclusive content. It's designed for writers, journalists, analysts, and creators who want to build direct relationships with their audience and generate sustainable income without relying on ads or sponsorships.

Is Substack good for beginners?

Substack is very beginner-friendly with an intuitive interface and minimal technical setup required. However, beginners should understand that growth relies partly on the platform's discovery algorithm and their own audience-building efforts, so initial growth may be slower than on platforms with more marketing automation tools.

Does Substack have an affiliate program?

No, Substack does not offer an affiliate program. This means you cannot earn commissions by referring new creators to the platform, which may be a limitation if partnership and referral-based growth is part of your strategy.
