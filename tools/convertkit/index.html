ConvertKit Review 2025: Everything You Need to Know

Freemium Free plan available From $29/mo G2: 4.4★

ConvertKit is email platform built for creators that positions itself for creators, bloggers, and course sellers. Founded in 2013, it has become one of the most recognised platforms in the email marketing space. In this review we cover what it does well, where it falls short, and who should actually use it.

Our verdict: ConvertKit is a solid choice for creators, bloggers, and course sellers. Its free tier is genuinely useful. The main trade-off is between its particular strengths and the limitations that make alternatives worth considering for some users.

Pros

  • Competitive pricing starting at $29/mo

  • Free tier available

Cons

  • Can get expensive at higher subscriber counts

ConvertKit offers a free plan, with paid plans from $29/month. Pricing typically scales with subscriber count.

Frequently asked questions

Is ConvertKit free?

Yes, ConvertKit has a free plan supporting up to 1000 subscribers. This makes it accessible for new senders testing the platform.

What is ConvertKit best used for?

ConvertKit is best for creators, bloggers, and course sellers. Its standout features include visual automations, subscriber tagging, landing pages, which suit this use case well.

Is ConvertKit good for beginners?

ConvertKit is generally beginner-friendly. The interface is straightforward for basic campaigns, though advanced automation features have a learning curve.

Does ConvertKit have an affiliate program?

Yes, ConvertKit runs an affiliate program paying 30% recurring for 24 months. This is worth considering if you plan to recommend the platform to your audience.
