Best Substack Alternatives for Newsletter Creators & Writers

While Substack excels at monetizing newsletters through a revenue-share model, it may not be the best fit for everyone. Whether you're looking for more advanced automation, better customer support, or a different pricing structure, several compelling alternatives offer unique advantages tailored to different creator needs and business models.

Why look for an alternative?
  • Superior customer support and higher satisfaction ratings than Substack's 2.9 Trustpilot score
  • More advanced automation, segmentation, and analytics features for growing audiences
  • Flexible pricing models with lower startup costs or flat fees instead of revenue sharing

Best Substack Alternatives in 2025

1
Beehiiv
The newsletter platform built for growth  ·  Best for: Newsletter publishers and media operators
Beehiiv is purpose-built for newsletter creators with built-in monetization options, an ad network, and referral programs—making it the closest direct competitor to Substack. It boasts the highest customer satisfaction among alternatives with a 4.4 Trustpilot rating and offers up to 2,500 free subscribers.

ConvertKit
Email platform built for creators  ·  Best for: Creators, bloggers, and course sellers
ConvertKit specializes in creator monetization with integrated commerce features, landing pages, and visual automations designed specifically for independent writers. With 1,000 free subscribers and recurring 30% affiliate commissions, it's ideal for creators wanting more control than Substack's revenue share.

3
MailerLite
Simple email marketing for small businesses  ·  Best for: Small businesses and freelancers on a budget
MailerLite offers the lowest entry price at just $9/month with unlimited free subscribers up to 1,000 contacts, plus drag-and-drop automation and pop-up tools. It's perfect for writers who want affordability and simplicity without Substack's revenue-sharing constraints.

ActiveCampaign
Email, automation, and CRM combined  ·  Best for: Businesses needing advanced automation and CRM
ActiveCampaign combines email marketing with enterprise-grade CRM, lead scoring, and dynamic content personalization for sophisticated automation workflows. It's the best choice for writers or creators who want to deeply segment audiences and track customer behavior beyond basic newsletter sending.

Brevo

Brevo stands out with an exceptional free tier supporting up to 100,000 contacts and offering multi-channel capabilities including SMS, WhatsApp, and live chat alongside email. It's ideal for creators wanting to reach audiences across multiple channels without the limitations of email-only platforms like Substack.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best free alternative to Substack?

Brevo offers the most generous free plan with up to 100,000 contacts, making it ideal for creators just starting out. If you need newsletter-specific features, Beehiiv provides a solid free tier with 2,500 subscribers and built-in monetization tools, while MailerLite offers unlimited free contacts up to 1,000 subscribers with more automation features.

What is the best Substack alternative for small business?

MailerLite is best for small businesses due to its affordability ($9/month), intuitive drag-and-drop editor, and practical features like pop-ups and surveys. For businesses needing CRM and advanced automation, ActiveCampaign ($15/month) provides superior customer management and lead scoring capabilities despite a slightly higher price point.

Is there a cheaper alternative to Substack?

Yes—MailerLite starts at just $9/month, making it cheaper than most Substack paid tiers while offering unlimited subscriber growth. Brevo is completely free for up to 100,000 subscribers with email sending, and GetResponse starts at $19/month with webinar and course hosting included, providing better value for creators than Substack's revenue-share model.
