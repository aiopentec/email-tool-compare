Best Substack Alternatives in 2025

While Substack offers a straightforward platform for monetizing newsletters, alternatives may better suit your needs depending on your audience size, automation requirements, or monetization strategy. Whether you're seeking more advanced features, better customer support, or lower revenue-share fees, these alternatives provide compelling options for writers, creators, and publishers.

Why look for an alternative?
  • Access more advanced automation and segmentation tools not available in Substack's core offering
  • Take advantage of lower fees or different pricing models that better suit your revenue goals
  • Leverage built-in ad networks and referral programs for additional monetization beyond subscriptions

Best Substack Alternatives in 2025

Beehiiv
The newsletter platform built for growth  ·  Best for: Newsletter publishers and media operators
Beehiiv directly competes with Substack but offers an integrated ad network and referral program for additional revenue streams beyond paid subscriptions. With higher free tier limits (2,500 subscribers) and superior customer ratings, it's ideal for publishers seeking growth-focused tools.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best free alternative to Substack?

Beehiiv offers the most generous free tier with up to 2,500 subscribers and built-in monetization features through its ad network. ConvertKit and MailerLite also have free plans supporting 1,000 subscribers each, though with slightly more limited features than Beehiiv.

What is the best Substack alternative for small business?

MailerLite is excellent for small businesses due to its affordable pricing starting at just $9/month combined with comprehensive features like landing pages, pop-ups, and e-commerce integrations. For businesses needing customer relationship management alongside email, ActiveCampaign provides integrated CRM functionality at a competitive price.

Is there a cheaper alternative to Substack?

Yes—MailerLite starts at $9/month compared to Substack's 10% revenue share on paid subscriptions, making it financially better for many publishers. GetResponse ($19/month) and Brevo ($25/month) also offer fixed pricing that may be cheaper depending on your subscriber count and revenue.
