Best Buttondown Alternatives for Email Marketing & Newsletters

While Buttondown excels for developers and technical writers who prefer markdown, it may not be the best fit for everyone. Whether you need more advanced automation, e-commerce integrations, or better monetization options, there are several powerful alternatives worth considering. We've compiled the top seven options to help you find the perfect email marketing platform for your specific needs.

Why look for an alternative?
  • Need more advanced automation and segmentation features beyond Buttondown's core offering
  • Require integrations with e-commerce platforms, CRM systems, or other business tools
  • Looking for monetization options, ad networks, or built-in commerce features for your newsletter

Best Buttondown Alternatives in 2025

1
MailerLite
Simple email marketing for small businesses  ·  Best for: small businesses and freelancers on a budget
MailerLite matches Buttondown's affordable $9 starting price while offering drag-and-drop editor, landing pages, and pop-ups—ideal if you prefer visual design over markdown. With 1,000 free subscribers and excellent customer support, it's perfect for non-technical users looking for Buttondown's affordability without the markdown requirement.

2
Beehiiv
The newsletter platform built for growth  ·  Best for: newsletter publishers and media operators
Beehiiv is purpose-built for newsletter publishers with built-in monetization, an ad network, and referral programs—features Buttondown lacks. If you want to turn your newsletter into revenue, Beehiiv's growth-focused tools and generous 2,500 free subscriber limit make it the superior choice.
Buttondown vs Beehiiv →
3
ConvertKit
Email platform built for creators  ·  Best for: creators, bloggers, and course sellers
ConvertKit offers visual automation builders, landing pages, and built-in commerce features designed specifically for creators and course sellers. If you need to sell digital products or manage complex subscriber journeys beyond email, ConvertKit's creator-focused ecosystem outpaces Buttondown's technical simplicity.
Buttondown vs ConvertKit →
4
ActiveCampaign
Email, automation, and CRM combined  ·  Best for: businesses needing advanced automation and CRM
ActiveCampaign combines email marketing with full CRM, lead scoring, and site tracking—offering enterprise-grade automation that Buttondown's simpler platform cannot match. If you need sophisticated marketing automation and customer relationship management integrated into one system, ActiveCampaign is the premium alternative.

5
Mailchimp
The all-in-one marketing platform  ·  Best for: beginners and small businesses
Mailchimp provides a free tier for up to 500 subscribers with robust features including A/B testing, audience segmentation, and landing pages. While it has lower user satisfaction ratings, its established ecosystem and free tier make it a solid choice for beginners wanting more marketing tools than Buttondown offers.
Buttondown vs Mailchimp →
6
Brevo
All-in-one CRM suite  ·  Best for: transactional email and multi-channel marketing
Brevo uniquely offers an enormous free tier with up to 100,000 subscribers, plus built-in SMS, WhatsApp, and live chat capabilities alongside email. If you need multi-channel communication and transactional email APIs at no cost, Brevo's unlimited free subscriber limit demolishes Buttondown's 100-subscriber restriction.
Buttondown vs Brevo →
7
GetResponse
Complete online marketing platform  ·  Best for: online course creators and webinar hosts
GetResponse integrates native webinar hosting, online course creation, and conversion funnel builders—features completely absent from Buttondown. If you're a course creator or run webinars, GetResponse's all-in-one platform eliminates the need for separate tools.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best free alternative to Buttondown?

Brevo offers the most generous free tier with up to 100,000 subscribers at no cost, making it ideal if you don't want to pay anything upfront. MailerLite and Beehiiv also provide robust free plans (1,000 and 2,500 subscribers respectively) with better features than Buttondown's 100-subscriber limit. However, if you specifically need markdown support and API access like Buttondown, you'll need to pay for most alternatives.

What is the best Buttondown alternative for small business?

MailerLite is the best alternative for small businesses, offering the same $9 starting price as Buttondown while providing drag-and-drop editing, landing pages, pop-ups, and excellent customer support. If you have a slightly larger budget, ConvertKit works well for service-based businesses selling digital products, while ActiveCampaign is ideal if you need CRM integration and advanced automation.

Is there a cheaper alternative to Buttondown?

Yes—Brevo is completely free for up to 100,000 subscribers, making it far more affordable than Buttondown's paid plans. MailerLite matches Buttondown's $9 starting price with more features included. If you want markdown support and paid subscription features at lower costs, Buttondown is actually quite competitive, but Brevo's free tier cannot be beaten for price.
